Why this role exists

This is not a Head of IT role. It's a builder role.

We have a Junior AI Developer in-house already — sharp, hungry, shipping his first automations. He needs a senior to learn from. We have ~50 people across the world running an e-commerce business at growth stage. This role sits at the center of how all of that gets reinvented over the next 3 years.

You will not manage SaaS contracts, write strategy decks, or run "digital transformation roadmaps." You will build the systems, enable the operations team to use them, and mentor a small team as it grows around you. This is one of the most important roles in the company right now — which is why you'll work directly with the CEO and the COO from day one.

The vision

Where we're going — and your three pillars to get us there.

Within 3–5 years, the majority of execution at CD Commerce should be handled by AI agents. Humans stay in the loop for decisions and for fine-tuning the agents, not for the work itself. That's the destination. Here's the road.

01

Knowledge Base

Vectorize the company's qualitative knowledge so AI can retrieve and act on it. Quantitative data already runs well via our analytics partner — you own the qualitative layer.

02

Agent Layer

Production AI agents per function — listings, supplier comms, marketing, customer service, internal retrieval. Not toy demos. Systems the team relies on every day.

03

Enablement Layer

Specialists already vibe-code small tools with Claude and Cursor. You turn their prototypes into robust production apps — proper databases, deployments, security. You enable 50 people to build with AI, safely.

What you'll do

Concrete milestones, not vague responsibilities.

First 90 days

Audit, prioritize, ship one

  • Audit the company's current AI and automation footprint.
  • Map the highest-leverage use cases. Pick the first two with leadership.
  • Ship one production agent that visibly removes a chunk of human work.
  • Sit with department leads. Learn how the workflows really run.
First 12 months

Build the foundation

  • Roll out v1 of the qualitative knowledge base across two departments.
  • Ship 3–5 production AI agents.
  • Mentor our Junior AI Developer into a strong builder.
  • Establish a simple build–test–deploy cycle, with security and access controls.
  • Set up the framework that lets specialists prototype with AI safely.
Year 2+

Scale and lead

  • Hire 1–2 more developers. Stay small, stay sharp.
  • Start on custom systems where SaaS no longer fits (likely WMS, ERP add-ons).
  • Step into AI Lead. Then Head of AI.

Self-selection

Be honest with yourself before you apply.

✕ Don't apply if…

  • You're optimizing for work–life balance. We're not asking you to work nights, but this role is for someone who treats this as a craft, not a 9-to-5.
  • You've never deployed something using an LLM in production. Tutorials don't count — side projects do.
  • Your strongest sentence about AI is "we need to be careful about hallucinations and security." We know. So do you. That's not a strategy.

  • You're hungry. You want to ship things, prove yourself fast, and grow into a leadership role on impact alone.
  • E-commerce, marketplaces, supply chains, brand-building — you find this space worth diving into for years.
  • You enable others to build, not just build alone. Mentoring a Junior, supporting specialists — that energizes you.
  • You want a place where the founder reads release notes too, and the COO trusts you to ship.
